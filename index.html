backwards.dev

Sometimes it seems like everyone on the internet is making money… except me.

Can you relate?

I failed at every web-based business I tried to build. Because I could never get started.

I would get nerd-sniped by insignificant technical decisions, obsess over designs that no one would ever see, distract myself by which shiny new technology to use, or convince myself that I couldn’t be successful unless I mastered XYZ first through a book or online course.

So now I’m going to try the opposite.

I’m going to do things backwards.

I’m going to start with the end — the famed checkout button — first, and then work my way backwards. All while staying on the cutting-edge of mediocrity.

I call it backwards development.

Start at the End, and then work your way backwards. This is the mantra.

There’s no product, no marketing campaign, no team, no plan. Just a checkout button.

It’s not much, but it’s a start. Or is the end?

I have nothing of value to offer you at the moment. Yet. Now my job is to work backwards to make the button below worth clicking.
