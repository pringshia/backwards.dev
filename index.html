backwards.dev

Sometimes it seems like everyone on the internet is making money… except me.

Can you relate?

I failed at every web-based business I tried to build. Because I could never get started.

I would get nerd-sniped by insignificant technical decisions, obsess over designs that no one would ever see, distract myself by which shiny new technology to use, convince myself that I couldn’t be successful unless I mastered XYZ first through a book or online course, or struggle with the emotional turmoil of asking for money.

So now I’m going to try the opposite.

I’m going to do things backwards.

I’m going to start with the end — the famed Pay Me button — first, and then work my way backwards. All while staying on the cutting-edge of mediocrity.

I call it backwards development.

Make the end the start, and then work my way backwards. This is the mantra.

There’s no product, no service, no marketing campaign, no team, no plan. Just a Pay Me button that works.

It’s not much, but it’s a start. Or is the end?

At the moment, I have nothing of value to offer you. Yet. Now my job is to work backwards to make the button below worth clicking.
